Museum Guide · Optional Late Evening

Fotografiska

Stockholm's contemporary photography powerhouse — 4–6 rotating shows, no permanent collection, open until 23:00. In a 1906 red-brick customs house on the Södermalm waterfront, with a celebrated rooftop bar facing Gamla Stan. Arrive ~21:00 in late June and the sun is still up. The optional, magical end to Day 5 — if energy allows.

The Short Answer

Worth it — if you still have energy.

Fotografiska holds no permanent collection and sells none of the work it shows; it runs large rotating exhibitions across the full range of photography, from documentary to experimental. The building, the shows and the rooftop combine into Stockholm's most consistently enjoyable cultural evening. But honestly weigh the day first: by 21:00 you'll have done Skansen, Waldemarsudde, a fika, dinner at Eld, the ferry crossing and a Gamla Stan golden-hour stroll — the day after the SOAD concert. If the family's energy is there, it's strongly worth it; if not, the Old Town stroll is already a lovely ending, and this is the optional layer.

On View · Verified for June 30, 2026

Five shows — here's the order to take them

📸 Martin Parr — "Short & Sweet" to 27 Sep

Britain's most recognisable documentary photographer: colour-saturated, deadpan observations of consumer culture and everyday life. A major name and typically a very strong show. → If you do only one, do this one.

🌀 Lebohang Kganye — "Le Sale ka Kgotso" to 18 Oct

South African artist at the intersection of photography, performance and collective memory — layered, dreamlike composites. → A single-artist show with a coherent, powerful body of work; Fotografiska at its best.

🪞 Lotta Antonsson — "I Am Everything" to 29 Nov

Swedish artist exploring gender, identity and objectification across photography, collage and spatial installation — unsettling and beautiful at once.

🤖 "The Machine's Eye in Human Hands" to 29 Nov

A thematic show on the camera, the machine and the human hand behind the image — the relationship between technology and the photographer.

🌓 Joakim Möller — "Time isn't holding us" to 23 Aug

An emerging-artist show: black-and-white photographs that turn the city's in-between spaces into poetic compositions of light and shadow.

Rotation warning: Fotografiska changes constantly — this list was verified on the official what's-on for June 30, but re-check the evening you go. Occasional private events close a floor for a few hours.
The Rooftop

The real reason to come late

The top-floor restaurant and bar is the venue's signature — and in midsummer, arriving at 21:00 means the sun is still above the horizon (sunset ~22:00). The panorama takes in Gamla Stan directly across the water (spires and rooflines in golden light), Djurgården to the right (the island you spent the day on), and the broad Saltsjön harbour with its ferries. The restaurant is award-winning and seasonal; it fills up in summer, so arrive by ~21:00 if you want to eat. The bar is less pressured — a drink with the view is the simpler way to end a long day.

Practical Notes

🎟️ Tickets & discounts

200 SEK (~€18) weekday / 230 weekend; students & seniors reduced (bring proof); under-16 free. A 2-for-1 every Wednesday from 18:00 — useful only if plans shift to a Wednesday.

📷 Photography & vibe

Photography is permitted and encouraged throughout. The venue is unusually relaxed and social for a museum — dogs are even welcome.

🔋 Energy check

This is the optional layer of an already-full day after the SOAD night. If anyone's flagging, the Gamla Stan stroll is a complete ending on its own — no guilt in skipping.
